Frequently Asked Questions

You should use ChronoPass because the average person has to remember over 80 different passwords, which is nearly impossible to do securely. Instead of reusing the same weak password everywhere (which is dangerous), ChronoPass lets you store unique, complex credentials for every site in a centralized, secure database. You only need to remember one 'Master Password,' and ChronoPass handles the rest.

ChronoPass acts as your centralized digital vault. It allows you to securely input and store your usernames, passwords, and notes for various websites in one protected database. Instead of struggling to remember login details for every site you visit, ChronoPass lets you organize them all in a single dashboard, ensuring you always have access to your credentials when you need them.

Yes. ChronoPass is designed to be far more secure than keeping passwords in a notebook or a sticky note. Your data is stored in a structured, local MySQL database that is isolated from the public internet. In a fully deployed environment, this 'digital vault' ensures that your sensitive information is organized and restricted, accessible only when you authenticate with your Master Password.

Due to security concerns, it’s not recommended to use the same password for all your accounts. If at least one of your accounts were to be breached, hacked, or otherwise compromised, that would make all your accounts vulnerable. This risk grows exponentially if you reuse the password for both personal and work-related accounts.

To access your stored credentials, simply navigate to the 'View Passwords' section of the ChronoPass dashboard. This secure area retrieves your encrypted data from the database and displays your list of saved websites and usernames in an organized table. For security, the actual passwords are hidden by default, but you can choose to reveal or copy them when needed.
